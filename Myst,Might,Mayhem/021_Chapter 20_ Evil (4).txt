Chapter 20: Evil (4)

The human skin that formed the surface of the book.

No one had ever thought of tearing it off and putting it on their own skin.

But Mok Gyeong-un had done exactly that.

The skin of the book, which was actually the skin of an ancient, powerful entity, now covered his own left arm.

It was a fusion of human and something far more primal.

The Demonic Monk watched in silent awe.

"It's... it's perfect," he whispered.

Mok Gyeong-un flexed his left arm.

The skin felt strange—cold, yet pulsing with a hidden, dark energy.

"Yes. It feels... right."

He looked at the Monk.

"Now, tell me. How do I use this power?"

The Monk's form flickered with excitement.

"You don't use it, Great One. You let it use you. You become the conduit for its will."

Mok Gyeong-un's eyes narrowed.

"I am no one's conduit. I am the master of my own fate."

"Of course, of course. But the power... it needs a purpose. And the purpose is the ritual."

"The Ritual of Attachment."

"Yes. The ritual that will bind this world to the next. The ritual that will bring back the old gods."

Mok Gyeong-un smiled.

"Then let the ritual begin. I am ready to meet these gods."

The game was no longer a mere adventure. It was a descent into the very heart of darkness. And Mok Gyeong-un was leading the way.
