Chapter 64: Gal-jeo (2)

Not far from the valley where the steel marble competition took place and the mountain where the flames of the ritual burned, the old tannery stood in silence.

Mok Gyeong-un entered the building, his eyes adjusting to the dim light.

The air was thick with the scent of blood and chemicals.

In the center of the room, a man was working on a large piece of leather.

He was a giant of a man, with muscles that seemed too large for his frame.

This was the Gal-jeo Butcher.

"You're not from around here, boy," the Butcher said without looking up.

"I am Mok Gyeong-un. And I am here to offer you a deal."

The Butcher laughed, a deep, booming sound that echoed through the tannery.

"A deal? And what could a scrawny boy like you offer me?"

"Power. The power to become more than just a butcher."

Mok Gyeong-un raised his left arm, and the dark energy surged forward.

The Butcher stopped his work and looked at the boy, his eyes widening in surprise.

"That... that's not human."

"No. It's the void. And it can be yours."

The Butcher was silent for a moment.

"And what do you want in exchange?"

"Your strength. Your skill. Your absolute loyalty."

The Butcher looked at the boy, then at his own hands.

"I accept. I am yours, Master."

Mok Gyeong-un smiled.

"Good. Now, let's begin the ritual."

The game was becoming a gathering. And Mok Gyeong-un was the one who was collecting the pieces.
