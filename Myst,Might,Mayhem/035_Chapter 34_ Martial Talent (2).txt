Chapter 34: Martial Talent (2)

-How dare a lowly mortal presume to examine my body by striking my acupoints without permission? Does he wish for death?

The Demonic Monk's voice was filled with a mixture of anger and amusement.

Mok Gyeong-un was still standing in the training courtyard, his eyes fixed on Sang Ung-baek.

The outer manor master was currently examining the boy's body, his fingers pressing against various pressure points.

"Master Sang... is something wrong?" Mok Gyeong-un asked in a neutral voice.

Sang Ung-baek didn't answer. He was too busy being confused.

The boy's meridians were... strange. They were wide and clear, as if they had been cultivated for years.

But he was also completely devoid of internal energy.

It was a contradiction that defied all logic.

"You... you've never learned martial arts before?" Sang Ung-baek finally asked, his voice full of doubt.

"No, Master. This is my first time."

Sang Ung-baek stood up, his face a mask of astonishment.

"This is... impossible. You possess the body of a once-in-a-century genius. But you have no energy to fill it."

Mok Gyeong-un smiled a small, hidden smile.

"Perhaps I'm just... special, Master."

The Monk laughed in his mind.

"Special? You are a monster, Great One. A vessel waiting to be filled with the darkness of the void."

The game was becoming a transformation. And Mok Gyeong-un was the one who was evolving.
