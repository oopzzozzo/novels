Chapter 33: Martial Talent (1)

A middle-aged man with a fierce appearance.

He was Sang Ung-baek, the outer manor master of Yeon Mok Sword Manor.

He was currently observing a group of young disciples as they practiced their sword forms in the training courtyard.

His eyes were sharp and critical.

He was looking for one thing: talent.

In the world of martial arts, talent was everything.

It was the difference between a mediocre warrior and a true master.

"Next!" he shouted.

A young boy stepped forward. He was thin and pale, with eyes that seemed too large for his face.

This was Mok Gyeong-un.

Sang Ung-baek watched as the boy took his stance.

His movements were slow and deliberate, almost hesitant.

"Start!"

Mok Gyeong-un began the first form of the Yeon Mok Sword Style.

It was a simple, basic form, but the boy performed it with a strange, almost hypnotic grace.

Sang Ung-baek's eyes narrowed.

There was something... different about this boy.

He lacked the raw strength and speed of the other disciples, but his technique was perfect.

Too perfect.

"Stop," Sang Ung-baek commanded.

Mok Gyeong-un froze in mid-swing.

"Where did you learn that?"

The boy looked at him, his eyes lifeless.

"I... I just watched the others, Master."

Sang Ung-baek was silent for a moment.

"Watched the others? And you learned it perfectly in one go?"

"Yes, Master."

Sang Ung-baek felt a chill run down his spine.

This boy had... exceptional talent. Or rather, he had something far more dangerous.

The game was becoming a trial. And Mok Gyeong-un was the one who was being tested.
