Chapter 35: Martial Talent (3)

Early the next morning.

One of the young servants in the medicine hall carefully brought Mok Gyeong-un his breakfast.

The boy was in his own room, sitting cross-legged on the floor.

He had been there all night, motionless and silent.

"Young Master... your breakfast," the servant whispered, setting the tray down.

Mok Gyeong-un opened his eyes. They were clear and focused, with no sign of fatigue.

"Thank you."

The servant quickly bowed and left the room.

The Demonic Monk appeared from the shadows.

"You've been meditating all night. Did you find what you were looking for?"

Mok Gyeong-un stood up and stretched.

"I found... a rhythm. A pulse that exists within the void."

"A pulse?"

"Yes. Like a heartbeat. And I've learned how to sync my own heartbeat with it."

He raised his left arm, and the skin of the book pulsed with a faint, dark light.

"When I do that, the energy of the void flows into me. Not as internal energy, but as something... else."

"And what does this 'something else' do?" the Monk asked, his voice full of curiosity.

"It... enhances. My senses, my strength, my very existence."

Mok Gyeong-un walked to the window and looked out at the manor.

"I am no longer a mere human. I am becoming something more. Something... divine."

The game was becoming a transformation. And Mok Gyeong-un was the one who was transcending.
